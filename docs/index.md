# Election Security Research

**Municipal ordinance campaign for enforcement of 18 USC 592 — protecting polling places from federal military and armed federal law enforcement intimidation.**

---

!!! abstract "About This Project"
    This platform provides 50-state legal analysis for a municipal ordinance campaign
    enforcing 18 USC 592, the federal statute prohibiting troops at polling places.
    The research supports a three-tier compliance architecture for lobbying registration
    across all 50 states, with litigation strategy for state court civil actions.
    This resource serves election protection advocates, municipal officials, and
    civil rights attorneys.

## Key Findings

1. **Municipal authority is viable**: Home rule cities in at least 30 states possess sufficient authority to adopt local ordinances reinforcing 18 USC 592 protections[^1].
2. **Six states lead in viability**: Illinois, Colorado, Massachusetts, Maryland, Oregon, and Rhode Island combine strong home rule provisions with favorable political conditions[^2].
3. **Three-tier compliance architecture**: A scalable model for 50-state lobbying registration — from DIY intelligence tools to managed filing services to emergency legal insurance[^3].
