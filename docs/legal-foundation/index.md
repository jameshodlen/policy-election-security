# Legal Foundation

## Overview

The municipal ordinance campaign rests on two pillars of federal law and the
intersection of state constitutional authority with local government power.

### Federal Statutory Basis

- **[18 USC 592](federal-law.md)** — Prohibits stationing troops at polling places on election day, with criminal penalties
- **[18 USC 1385](federal-law.md#posse-comitatus-act)** — The Posse Comitatus Act restricting use of military for civilian law enforcement

### State Authority Framework

- **[Home Rule vs. Dillon's Rule](state-authority.md)** — How state constitutional frameworks determine municipal authority to adopt reinforcing ordinances

## Strategic Architecture

The campaign targets approximately 180 cities across all 50 states, organized by viability tier:

| Tier | Viability | Example States | Target Cities |
|------|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Tier 1 | High | IL, CO, MA, MD, OR, RI | ~60 |
| Tier 2 | Moderate | MN, WA, PA, CA, NM, CT | ~80 |
| Tier 3 | Low | ID, SD, WY | ~40 |

See [State Guides](../state-guides/index.md) for jurisdiction-specific analysis.
