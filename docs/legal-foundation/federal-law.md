# Federal Law

## 18 USC 592 — Troops at Polling Places

!!! quote "Statutory Text"
    Whoever, being an officer of the Army or Navy, or other person in the civil,
    military, or naval service of the United States, orders, brings, keeps, or has
    under his authority or control any troops or armed men at any place where a
    general or special election is held, unless such force be necessary to repel
    armed enemies of the United States, shall be fined under this title or
    imprisoned not more than five years, or both; and be disqualified from
    holding any office of honor, profit, or trust under the United States.

### Key Elements

1. **Broad scope**: Applies to any person "in the civil, military, or naval service of the United States"
2. **Election day protection**: Covers any place where a general or special election is held
3. **Limited exception**: Only "armed enemies of the United States" justifies military presence
4. **Severe penalties**: Up to five years imprisonment plus permanent disqualification from federal office

### Enforcement Gap

Despite its clear prohibitions, 18 USC 592 has seen minimal federal enforcement. This enforcement gap creates the rationale for municipal reinforcement — local ordinances that create parallel civil enforcement mechanisms.

## Posse Comitatus Act (18 USC 1385) {: #posse-comitatus-act }

!!! quote "Statutory Text"
    Whoever, except in cases and under circumstances expressly authorized by the
    Constitution or Act of Congress, willfully uses any part of the Army or the
    Air Force as a posse comitatus or otherwise to execute the laws shall be
    fined under this title or imprisoned not more than two years, or both.

### Relationship to 18 USC 592

The Posse Comitatus Act provides broader restrictions on military involvement in civilian law enforcement, while 18 USC 592 specifically targets the election context. Together, they establish a comprehensive federal framework against militarized election interference.

### Limitations

- Does not apply to the Navy or Marine Corps by statute (though DoD policy extends the principle)
- Does not apply to the National Guard when acting under state authority (Title 32)
- Insurrection Act (10 USC 251-255) provides exceptions
